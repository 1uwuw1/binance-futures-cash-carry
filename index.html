ABOUT CASH & CARRY STRATEGY

WHAT IS CASH & CARRY?

CASH AND CARRY IS A STRATEGY WHERE AN INVESTOR BUYS THE UNDERLYING ASSET (SPOT CRYPTO) AND SIMULTANEOUSLY SELLS A CORRESPONDING FUTURES CONTRACT. PROFIT COMES FROM THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN THE FUTURES PRICE AND THE SPOT PRICE WHEN FUTURES TRADE HIGHER THAN SPOT.

HOW TO INTERPRET YIELDS

POSITIVE YIELDS INDICATE THE FUTURES ARE TRADING AT A PREMIUM TO SPOT AND ARE THEREFORE IN CONTANGO. NEGATIVE YIELDS SUGGEST THE MARKET IS IN BACKWARDATION (FUTURES BELOW SPOT). YIELDS ARE CALCULATED BY ASSUMING YOU CROSS THE BID/ASK SPREAD TO OPEN POSITIONS AND INCORPORATE DERIBIT TRADING FEES. ANNUALIZED YIELDS SHOW WHAT THE RETURN WOULD BE IF THE POSITION COULD BE HELD FOR A FULL YEAR.

NOTE: THIS CALCULATOR MAY BE GROSSLY INNACURATE AND IS INTENDED FOR ENTERTAINMENT ONLY. SPOT BID/ASK PRICES ARE BASED ON DERIBIT PERPETUAL FUTURES PRICES, WHICH MAY DIFFER FROM TRUE SPOT PRICES. IF THE MARKET IS IN BACKWARDATION NEGATIVE YIELDS DO NOT REPRESENT THE POTENTIAL PROFIT FROM BORROWING SPOT AND BUYING FUTURES.
